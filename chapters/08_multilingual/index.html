Chapter 8: Multilinguality

Multilingual applications such as machine translation, e.g. Google Translate, are ubiquitous and make our lives easier. Thus, in this chapter we discuss the topic multilingual natural language processing, with a focus on multilingual word embeddings and multilingual tranformers.

  • Chapter 8.1: Why Multilinguality?

    So far, we have seen interesting use cases for NLP. Clearly, these can be extended to multiple languages. But there are also interesting problems across languages. In this part we present a general motivation and a classification of problem types.

  • Chapter 8.2: Cross-lingual Word Embeddings

    In this session, multilingual word embeddings are discussed. We describe the two main training strategies and look at examples of models to train those. Additionally, we look at unsupervised learning of multi-lingual word embeddings.

  • Chapter 8.3: (Massively) Multilingual transformers

    As we have previously seen, transformers are the working horse for modern NLP. In this section we learn how transformers are adapted to work in multilingual settings and discuss typical issues when training those. Furthermore we look at zero-shot cross lingual transfer capabilities.
