Chapter 4.3: Early Transfer Learning architectures

Before the use of the Transformer architecture (cf. Chapter 5) for Transfer Learning, two milestone architectures were published.

Their importance stems from two pioneering paradigms they introduced: ELMo was the first model to learn word embeddings which embraces contextuality in a bidirectional way, meaning that the vector representation of a word depends on both the context on its left as well as the context on its right side. The other architecture, ULMFiT, is not only about creating embeddings but (pre-)training a whole architecture which can be adapted to data from the target task or domain.
